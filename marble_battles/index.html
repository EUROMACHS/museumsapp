The Marble Battles

You are a Greek journalist browsing YouTube. And you stumble upon the IQSquared debate about the Parthenon marble reunification...

This brings back so many memories... You have written about this issue a lot in the past. You are a firm believer that the precious marble sculptures, which had been removed from the Parthenon (mostly by the British), belong in Athens for two reasons.

First, these marbles are organic elements of a single artistic unit, and thus would be better appreciated as a whole. For instance, it would seem nonsensical to split the Rosetta's stone into fragments and scatter them around the globe - even though it is considered cultural heritage of the whole of humanity.

Second, the historical and cultural context - unlike physical objects such as the sculptures - cannot be replicated. And the removal of an art work from its original location results in a loss of 'aura' (or authenticity), as Walter Benjamin suggested. Hence, seen on the Acropolis, the marbles would be better understood. Furthermore, the presence of sculptures would permit a better setting for the interpretation of the Parthenon itself.

And even though the legality of the British Museum's possession of these marbles is questionable, the British are still reluctant to return them.

You blood starts to boil as you think of Greece's countless attempts to get the pieces of their cultural heritage back.

Start a quest to get the marbles back!
